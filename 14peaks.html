The 14 Peaks: Earth's Highest Challenges

Mount Everest — 8,849 m

Extreme altitude, avalanches, unpredictable weather.

K2 — 8,611 m

Technical routes, severe storms, high fatality rate.

Kangchenjunga — 8,586 m

Remote approaches, avalanche danger.

Lhotse — 8,516 m

Steep faces and high altitude challenges.

Makalu — 8,485 m

Technical climbing and mixed terrain.

Cho Oyu — 8,188 m

High altitude but considered one of the more accessible 8k peaks.

Dhaulagiri — 8,167 m

Avalanche-prone slopes and severe weather.

Manaslu — 8,163 m

Remote routes and crevasse hazards.

Nanga Parbat — 8,126 m

Dangerous headwall climbs and unpredictable weather.

Annapurna I — 8,091 m

High avalanche risk and technical sections.

Gasherbrum I — 8,080 m

Remote Karakoram environment and technical climbs.

Broad Peak — 8,051 m

Long ridges and high altitude fatigue.

Gasherbrum II — 8,035 m

Karakoram storms and remote logistics.

Shishapangma — 8,027 m

Often accessed from Tibet; altitude and crevasses.
