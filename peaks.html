14 High Peaks — Challenges & Stories

A curated list of legendary high-altitude climbs with brief notes and challenges.

Everest (8848 m)

The highest mountain on Earth. Weather and altitude are the main challenge.

K2 (8611 m)

Known as 'Savage Mountain' — technical and dangerous.

Kangchenjunga (8586 m)

Remote and sacred — unpredictable weather.

Lhotse (8516 m)

Close to Everest with steep final sections.

Makalu (8485 m)

Steep slopes and knife-edge ridges.

Cho Oyu (8188 m)

One of the more accessible 8000m peaks.

Dhaulagiri (8167 m)

Avalanche-prone slopes and strong winds.

Manaslu (8163 m)

Remote approaches and crevassed glaciers.

Nanga Parbat (8126 m)

Steep faces and technical climbs.

Annapurna (8091 m)

Highest fatality rate among 8000ers.

Gasherbrum I (8080 m)

Challenging weather and high winds.

Broad Peak (8051 m)

Exposed ridges and sudden storms.

Gasherbrum II (8035 m)

Requires careful acclimatization.

Shishapangma (8027 m)

Easier access but still a true high-altitude challenge.
